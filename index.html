Team Chiron works towards making remote autonomous triage a reality.

We build robotic systems to identify and characterize casualties and monitor vital signals and conditions before first responders are dispatched.

Our team, based at Carnegie Mellon University, is competing in DARPA's Triage challenge.

Our work is supported by our sponsors. Will you become the next?

Contact Us

Our solution characterizes human casualties through sensor fusion in adverse environments.

Optical & infrared cameras scan the human body for injuries and bodily condition.

Radar & laser vibrometry measure heart and breathing rates.

Combining advanced processing with off-the-shelf sensors forms a low-risk high-reward system that is applicable to a wide range of users.
